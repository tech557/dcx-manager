## Prior art compliance

RS-R10 correctly inherits the RS-R0b §11 disposition policy: no direct deletion, file-by-file disposition rows, PO approval, and archive under `docs/archive/`. It also uses the RS-R5 source inventory groups and counts as its starting manifest.

The draft partially drops the newer calibration-mode rule. Under `REQ-GOV-CAL-001`, imperfect graph data can be carried as visible cleanup debt; therefore RS-R10 should not demand perfect graph certainty before producing a disposition proposal. At the same time, under `REQ-GOV-SRC-001` and `REQ-GOV-INV-001`, it must not treat inferred graph links as automatic authorization to erase or rewrite source evidence. The right revision is not to weaken RS-R10, but to make the disposition table evidence-rich and PO-gated, and to keep historical/provenance references immutable.

## Handoff quality

The handoff is close, but not safe yet. The output paths are named, the executor is named, and the PO gate is explicit. However, the next agent could follow Step 6 literally and rewrite historical logs, audit outputs, and graph provenance fields. The disposition table also does not carry enough graph replacement detail for the PO to confirm rows without reopening source files. Fixing the allowed-path scope and adding proof columns will make the sprint executable.
